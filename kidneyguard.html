The Problem

Why kidneys, and why now?

Chronic Kidney Disease is the third fastest-growing cause of death globally, projected to become the 5th leading cause of life-years lost by 2040. The tragedy is that most damage happens silently — long before any symptoms appear.

How might we integrate AI into modern medicine to detect kidney disease at its earliest stages to dramatically increase patient survival rates in the aging population?

🫁

What is CKD?

Chronic Kidney Disease occurs when kidneys gradually lose their ability to filter waste from the blood. It is classified into 5 stages — and by the time symptoms appear, significant damage has already occurred.

🔬

FSGS: The Root Cause

Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) is a key driver of CKD. Scarring on the kidney's glomeruli causes vital proteins to leak into urine — a process that is silent but detectable through biomarkers early on.

👴

The Aging Population

Kidney function naturally declines with age. 34% of adults over 65 have CKD. For those over 70, the compounding effects of hypertension, diabetes, and reduced GFR make early detection critical.

🤖

AI as the Solution

AI can detect patterns in biomarker data that humans cannot. By analysing blood pressure, glucose, and urinary protein levels continuously, machine learning models can predict CKD risk months before clinical diagnosis.

📉

Current Treatment Gaps

Standard markers like serum creatinine only rise after 40–50% of kidney tissue is already damaged. Current care is reactive — patients are diagnosed late, treated late, and outcomes suffer as a result.

💡

Our Approach

KidneyGuard shifts care from reactive to proactive. A wearable device continuously monitors key biomarkers and feeds data to an AI system that flags risk early — giving patients and doctors time to act.

The Device

KidneyGuard

A wearable patch worn on the wrist. Designed for simplicity — no setup, no configuration, no technical knowledge required. Built for people over 70 who may not own a smartphone or smartwatch.

Hardware

Built for simplicity. Engineered for precision.

Process

How KidneyGuard works

Four steps from wrist to diagnosis. The entire system runs automatically — no button presses, no manual logging required.

1

Continuous Sensing

KidneyGuard's sensors read blood pressure, heart rate variability, glucose levels, and bioimpedance every 60 seconds.

2

On-Device AI

A machine learning model trained on 621+ research studies analyses sensor data locally, looking for patterns associated with early CKD.

3

Cloud Sync

De-identified data is sent to the cloud where a larger AI model runs eGFR prediction and flags longitudinal trends that short-term sensors can't catch.

4

GP Alert

When risk scores exceed safe thresholds, an automatic alert is sent to the patient's registered GP for early intervention — potentially years before symptoms appear.

Ethical Considerations

Built responsibly

🔒

Data Privacy

All biometric data is de-identified before cloud transmission. Patients own their data. The system is designed to meet Australian Privacy Act standards and TGA regulatory requirements.

⚖️

Equitable Access

The device must be accessible across income levels and geographic locations. Subsidised distribution through the PBS and My Aged Care pathway is part of the proposed rollout model.

🧠

AI Transparency

Explainable AI (XAI) is used so doctors understand why the device raised an alert. The AI supports, but never replaces, clinical decision-making.
