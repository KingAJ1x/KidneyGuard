KidneyGuard: Early Chronic Kidney Disease Detection Concept

Welcome to our iSTEM group project website. KidneyGuard is an idea we came up with for a simple wearable wrist patch designed for elderly people over 70. Our project looks at how basic sensors and data tracking could help spot early signs of kidney issues before they get too serious.

Project Background

Why We Chose This Topic

Chronic Kidney Disease is a major health issue that is growing quickly around the world. During our initial research phase, our group found that most early damage to the kidneys happens silently. Usually, doctors only find out when standard blood tests show a spike in waste products, but that often doesn't happen until a lot of the kidney tissue is already permanently damaged.

What is CKD?

Chronic Kidney Disease means the kidneys slowly lose their ability to clean waste out of the blood properly. It has 5 different stages, and because it doesn't hurt, people don't notice it until the later stages.

The Problem With Current Testing

Right now, regular screening requires patients to go into a lab for blood and urine tests. Elderly people living far away from clinics or who don't get regular checkups often miss these entirely.

Helping Older Patients

Kidney health naturally drops as people get older. Over 30% of adults over 65 have some level of kidney issues. High blood pressure and diabetes make this risk even worse for people over 70.

Device Concept

How the Wrist Patch is Setup

We designed this wearable idea to be as simple as possible. It is intended for older users who might not have a smartphone or understand how to configure complicated modern smartwatches.

Key Features of Our Idea:

Our design tries to solve the problem by tracking general health stats over time, looking for long-term patterns that show a user might need to visit their local GP for an actual checkup.

  • Tracks basic vitals like heart rate variations and skin temperature.
  • Uses a basic skin-contact sensor setup that doesn't require needles.
  • Runs a simple calculation script built directly onto the band microchip.
  • Planned to use a simple battery that lasts a week so they don't have to charge it constantly.
  • Built out of water-resistant silicone so they can wear it in the shower safely.

Process

Proposed Data Flow

This is the 4-step process we mapped out for how the device would collect and share data.

01

Reading Vitals

The skin sensors check heart rates, fluid retention signs, and temperature trends automatically every minute.

02

Checking Stats

A small processor chip inside the wristband compares these daily numbers against normal medical baselines.

03

Syncing Data

The band saves historical logs and syncs them to a companion application to watch for multi-week drops in vital trends.

04

Sending Alert

If numbers look consistently out of range, the app sends a warning note recommending that the user schedules a clinic checkup.
