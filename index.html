Project Proposal 2026

KidneyGuard: Early Chronic Kidney Disease Detection

For our Year 10 iSTEM assessment, our group designed a concept for a wearable wrist patch tailored for elderly citizens over 70. Our goal is to use non-invasive biometric sensors paired with a basic machine learning algorithm to catch signs of early-stage Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) before physical symptoms become severe.

Part A: Literature Review

Why Did We Choose Kidney Disease?

Chronic Kidney Disease is a major health crisis globally, especially among aging demographics. The core issue we identified during our initial research phase is that most early damage to the kidneys happens silently. Standard clinical markers, such as evaluating serum creatinine, often only show noticeable spikes after 40 to 50 percent of functional kidney tissue has suffered permanent damage.

Our Core Design Inquiry Question:

"How might we integrate basic AI principles into modern personal wearables to track potential kidney degradation early, thereby supporting preventative GP medical care in local clinical contexts?"

What is FSGS?

Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis is a condition involving localized scarring within kidney filtering units. This causes critical blood proteins to steadily escape into urine, which our device tries to predict via longitudinal systemic changes.

The Gaps in Current Care

Current screening models are predominantly reactive. Elderly patients living remotely or outside regular clinical checkups rarely complete routine urinalysis until symptoms materialize at advanced stages.

Part C: System Operational Flowchart

Proposed Data Pipeline

Step 1

Sensors scan blood pressure vectors and systemic fluid volume tracking trends every 60 seconds.

Step 2

Local microprocessor compares biometric values against baseline clinical reference statistics.

Step 3

Anonymized telemetry sets sync to local network clouds to check for subtle multi-week variations.

Step 4

If the safety threshold breaches, the application flags a warning note to prompt a medical checkup.

Ethics & Practical Constraints Checklist

As part of our project criteria, we must evaluate social and economic boundaries. The prototype relies entirely on secure data principles where user bio-records remain disconnected from commercial identifiers. Furthermore, to make this an equitable healthcare option, our deployment strategy envisions distribution through regional medical networks rather than relying on direct private retail sales.
